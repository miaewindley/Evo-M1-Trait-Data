# Smaers et al. 2011 — Supplementary Table 1 (RAW per-individual frontal volumes)

Smaers JB, Steele J, Case CR, Cowper A, Amunts K, Zilles K (2011). *Primate prefrontal cortex
evolution: human brains are the extreme of a lateralized ape trend.* Brain Behav Evol 77(2):67-78.
doi:10.1159/000323671

Full title (`__ReadMe.xlsx`): **"Supplementary Table 1. Volumes (cm3) of frontal white matter and frontal
grey matter in left and right hemispheres, and total brain volume. Numbers after species names refer to
catalogue numbers in the collection."**

## Source -> Snapshot (traceable)
**Source = `000323671_sm_suppltables.pdf`**, via its Adobe-Acrobat Excel export
**`000323671_sm_suppltables.xlsx`** (sheet `Table 1` = this table's data; sheet `Table 2` = its caption,
which Acrobat split onto a separate sheet — that is why the "topic" looked cut off). The snapshot is now
regenerated by **`Smaers_etal_2011_SupplementaryTable_extract.R`** (Adobe export -> snapshot, supplying
the printed column headers), so the source->snapshot step is reproducible — it is no longer a hand-typed
table. 26 individuals; left/right frontal white & grey + total brain; catalogue numbers in the Individual
label. **RAW, primary, per-individual data; left & right hemispheres kept separate.**

## Checking -> `comparison/`
`Smaers_etal_2011_compare_to_public_tsv.R` audits the snapshot against the DOI-coded TSV that **pre-existed
in the Public folder** (`comparison/10.1159%2F000323671_SupplementaryTable1.tsv`, prepared elsewhere),
matched by Individual, compared by position (its header is a collapsed multi-row header). **Result: 26/26
identical.** (Suppl. Table 2's pre-existing TSV was *not* — see that ReadMe.)

## Data readable
`..._SupplementaryTable1.R` -> `..._SupplementaryTable1.csv` (use this): species + catalogue split out;
`_total` = left + right. cm3.

## Provenance
Primary measurements (Smaers, with Zilles, C. & O. Vogt Institute). Ape individuals (`Pongo pygmaeus
yn85 38`, `Gorilla gorilla 375`, `Pan troglodytes 280`, `Pan paniscus 86137`, `Hylobates lar 1203`) are
the **same Hirnforschung / Stephan-Collection brains** as MacLeod 2003 & Barger 2007. With Smaers 2010
(PLoS One), this is the RAW source behind the frontal data reused in Smaers 2017.
